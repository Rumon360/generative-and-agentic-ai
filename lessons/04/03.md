## Let’s Understand Transformers

The term _“Transformer”_ originates from a research paper by Google titled _Attention Is All You Need_.

Transformers are the foundational architecture behind modern Large Language Models (LLMs). They enable models to process and understand language efficiently through mechanisms such as _attention_, which allows the model to focus on relevant parts of the input.

This paper introduced the Transformer architecture, which serves as the core foundation for models like GPT.

---

## How It Works

At a high level, a Transformer takes an input **sequence** and produces an output **sequence**. It does this by learning patterns from large amounts of data using machine learning.

One of the earliest large-scale applications of Transformers was in Google Translate. For example, it can take an English sentence as input and generate its equivalent in Spanish. The model learns how to map one sequence (English) to another (Spanish) based on training data.

---

## How GPT Is Different

Models like GPT work a bit differently.

A GPT model is a Transformer that generates text by **predicting the next token** in a sequence. Instead of translating between languages, it generates text step by step.

Here’s a simple example:

- Input: _“Hey there!”_
- Step 1 → Predict next token: _“I”_
- Step 2 → Input becomes: _“Hey there! I”_ → Predict: _“am”_
- Step 3 → Input becomes: _“Hey there! I am”_ → Predict: _“good.”_
- Step 4 → Model predicts an end token: _`<END>`_

Final output: **“I am good.”**

At each step, the model only predicts **one token at a time**, and the newly generated token is appended to the input. This process repeats until a stopping condition (like an end token) is reached.

---

## Key Idea

A Transformer-based model like GPT works by repeatedly answering one question:

> _“Given everything so far, what is the most likely next token?”_

It relies entirely on patterns learned during training.

---

## Why It Requires Powerful Hardware

This process is computationally intensive. Transformers perform large-scale matrix operations and attention calculations, which makes them much more efficient on GPUs than CPUs. That’s why modern LLMs typically require powerful GPU hardware for training and inference.
