## Understanding Large Language Models (LLMs)

**Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems** are computer programs designed to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. They can analyze data, recognize patterns, make predictions, understand language, interpret images or speech, and even control devices or automate processes.

**Large Language Models (LLMs)** are a type of AI system specifically designed to work with human language. They are trained on massive amounts of text data to learn patterns, structures, and relationships within language.

While LLMs do not understand language like humans, they can generate coherent and contextually relevant text. This allows them to answer questions, summarize information, translate languages, write code, and perform other language-based tasks.

In essence, LLMs **predict the next word or phrase** in a sequence based on what they have learned, enabling them to produce outputs that appear intelligent and human-like.
